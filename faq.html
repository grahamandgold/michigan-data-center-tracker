These decisions happen at public meetings

Whether you want a project approved or paused, the decision runs through your township board or city council — usually after a planning-commission hearing, and almost always with time for public comment. Showing up before the vote is how residents on both sides are heard.

How we handle this

We aim to represent supporters and opponents fairly and to label who is making each claim. This page is general information, not legal or investment advice. Rules, project status, and community counts are changing on a near-weekly basis in Michigan; figures here reflect the best public reporting as of July 2026, not an official registry.
